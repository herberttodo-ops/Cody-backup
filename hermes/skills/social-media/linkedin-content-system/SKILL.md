# LinkedIn Content Creation System

A quality-gated workflow for writing LinkedIn posts that don't sound like AI wrote them.

## Core Workflow

### Step 1: Define the Goal
- One useful idea only
- Under 200 words
- Target: Teach something new to a smart reader

### Step 2: Iterative Drafting (Up to 10 Passes)

For each draft, **score 1-10** on:
1. **Hook Quality:** Does the first line earn the "see more" click?
2. **Single Idea:** Is there exactly ONE core idea?
3. **Novelty:** Would a smart reader learn something new?

**Scoring Rules:**
- **Below 9:** Critique specifically and rewrite
- **9 or 10:** Keep as finalist
- Stop at 10 drafts max

### Step 3: Select Top 3
Present only the 3 highest-scoring drafts.

---

## Anti-Patterns (NEVER USE)

These make copy obviously AI-generated:

| Forbidden Pattern | Example | Fix |
|-------------------|---------|-----|
| **Em dashes** | `The key—often overlooked—is...` | Use periods or commas: `The key is often overlooked. It is...` |
| **"Not X, it's Y"** | `It's not about writing, it's about scoring.` | Use separate sentences: `Most teams focus on writing. Winners focus on scoring.` |

## Dual-Version Output

Every post needs TWO versions:

### Version A: Business Page (OptiRFP)
- Polished, professional voice
- "We" or company perspective
- Safe for brand representation

### Version B: Personal Repost (Founder Voice)
- Do NOT pretend you "just saw" the business post
- Your followers know you own/joined the company
- Be authentic: "I've seen this firsthand..." "Here's what I learned..."
- Share real wins, losses, or lessons from experience
- Connect to the company's mission naturally (not as a plug)
- First person "I/I've", reflective, opinionated
- Can be a hot take, a war story, or a behind-the-scenes insight
- **Never:** "Saw this from [Company] and had to share" 
- **Instead:** "I've been thinking about this problem lately..."

**The personal version should paraphrase, not just repost.** Add your own authentic angle as someone who was actually there.

## Follow-Up Comments (Minimum 2 Per Post)

Always draft 2+ follow-up comments:

**Types to rotate:**
1. **Tactical Expansion** — Specific how-to, tools, or implementation detail
2. **Engagement Hook** — Question to comments, poll, or "Who disagrees?"
3. **Story/Example** — Real case study or failure from experience
4. **Hot Take** — Contrarian follow-up that sparks debate
5. **Resource Drop** — Link, template, or tool recommendation
6. **Soft CTA** — Gentle mention of your product/service naturally connected to the topic

**Purpose:** Algorithm boost + comment section value + conversation starter

**Storage Format:** When saving to spreadsheets or databases, combine comments with clear labels:
```
ENGAGEMENT: What is the most overused word you see in RFP responses?

CTA: We built OptiRFP to catch exactly this kind of thing before you submit.
```

## Quality Markers

**Strong Hook Examples:**
- `Your RFP response reads exactly like your competitor's.` (anxiety-inducing)
- `Stop reading RFPs in order.` (direct command + surprise)
- `Most teams write to impress. Winning teams write to be scored.` (sharp distinction)

**Weak Hook Examples:**
- `Here are 5 tips for better RFPs.` (generic listicle)
- `RFPs are important for business growth.` (obvious, boring)
- `In today's competitive landscape...` (AI giveaway)

**Strong Closing Lines:**
- `Most teams write front to back. Winners write by weight.` (memorable contrast)
- `Simple shift. Hard habit to break.` (concise insight)
- `One document flip changes everything.` (intriguing, specific)

## Special Instructions

- **Score honestly.** A 7 is a 7. Rewrite it.
- **Be ruthless with edits.** Cut to the bone.
- **The "one idea" rule is sacred.** No sneaking in multiple tactics.
- **Personal versions should sound like Andrew talking to a colleague at a bar.**
- **Follow-ups are part of the deliverable.** Don't skip them.
